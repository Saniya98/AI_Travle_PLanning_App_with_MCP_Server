Based on the provided information, I've created a 7-day travel itinerary for Japan under $2000, including flight, hotel, and everything.

**Day 1: Arrival in Tokyo**

* Fly from the US to Tokyo, Japan (round-trip airfare: $600-$1,200)
* Arrive at Tokyo Narita or Haneda airport
* Check-in to a budget-friendly hotel in Tokyo (e.g., Sakura Hotel Ikebukuro, $30-$50 per night)
* Explore the nearby area, visit the Tokyo Metropolitan Government Building for a free observation deck with great city views
* Try some local street food for dinner (e.g., ramen, udon, or tempura)

**Day 2: Tokyo**

* Visit the famous Tsukiji Outer Market for sushi and fresh seafood (free admission)
* Take a stroll in the beautiful Imperial Palace East Garden (free admission)
* Explore the Asakusa district, including Senso-ji Temple (free admission, but donations welcome)
* Enjoy a traditional Japanese dinner at a local restaurant (e.g., yakitori, $20-$30 per person)

**Day 3: Tokyo**

* Visit the Meiji Shrine, a serene Shinto shrine located in a tranquil forested area (free admission)
* Take a walk in the trendy Harajuku district, known for its unique fashion and snack shops
* Visit the Ghibli Museum, dedicated to the works of Studio Ghibli and its founder Hayao Miyazaki ( tickets: $20 per person)
* Enjoy a traditional Japanese tea ceremony at the Happo-en Garden (tickets: $20-$30 per person)

**Day 4: Nikko World Heritage Site**

* Take a day trip to Nikko, a UNESCO World Heritage site located about two hours away from Tokyo by train (train tickets: $20-$30 per person, round-trip)
* Visit the Toshogu Shrine, a beautiful Shinto shrine dedicated to Tokugawa Ieyasu (admission: $10 per person)
* Explore the Rinno-ji Temple, a historic Buddhist temple with a beautiful garden (admission: $5 per person)
* Return to Tokyo in the evening and enjoy a local dinner

**Day 5: Kyoto**

* Travel to Kyoto by bullet train (Shinkansen, tickets: $100-$150 per person, one-way)
* Check-in to a budget-friendly hotel in Kyoto (e.g., Guest House Kiyomizu, $30-$50 per night)
* Visit the famous Kinkaku-ji Temple (Golden Pavilion, admission: $5 per person)
* Explore the nearby Nijo Castle, a UNESCO World Heritage site (admission: $5 per person)
* Enjoy a traditional Kyoto-style dinner (e.g., kaiseki, $30-$50 per person)

**Day 6: Kyoto**

* Visit the Fushimi Inari Shrine, famous for its thousands of vermillion torii gates (free admission)
* Take a stroll through the beautiful Arashiyama Bamboo Grove (free admission)
* Visit the Kiyomizu-dera Temple, a UNESCO World Heritage site and one of the most famous temples in Japan (admission: $5 per person)
* Enjoy a local dinner and relax in the evening

**Day 7: Departure**

* Spend the morning shopping for last-minute souvenirs or visiting any last-minute sights
* Depart for the airport and fly back to the US
